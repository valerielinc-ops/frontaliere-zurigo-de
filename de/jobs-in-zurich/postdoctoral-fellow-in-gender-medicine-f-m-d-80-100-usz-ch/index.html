Rollenüberblick

The Chair for Gender Medicine at the University Hospital Zurich and University of Zurich invites applications for an experienced and highly motivated postdoctoral researcher to help further develop an interdisciplinary research program in Gender Medicine.

We are looking for an outstanding postdoctoral researcher with a strong quantitative profile and broad experience in data science, epidemiology, public/global health or related fields. The successful candidate will play a central role in shaping an ambitious research agenda at the intersection of Gender Medicine, population health, clinical evidence generation and data analytics.
The University Hospital of Zurich is one of the largest hospitals in Switzerland. Our more than 8, 600 employees and around 700 trainees work every day for the well-being of our patients.

Aufgaben

You will lead and develop a portfolio of interdisciplinary research projects that investigate sex- and gender-related gaps and potential solutions in biomedical research, clinical evidence, disease burden, health outcomes and health policy.

Responsibilities include

  • Develop high-impact research on sex- and gender-sensitive medicine, clinical evidence gaps, global disease burden patterns, and opportunities for research prioritization and health equity
  • Build and apply large-scale, reproducible data pipelines
  • Use advanced quantitative methods, potentially including epidemiology, biostatistics, causal inference, machine learning, natural language processing, large language models, bibliometrics/scientometrics and real-world evidence methods
  • Translate research findings into peer-reviewed publications, policy briefs, reports, conference presentations and stakeholder-facing outputs
  • Contribute to competitive grant applications and help establish a sustainable externally funded research program
  • Coordinate interdisciplinary collaborations within UZH, USZ and national and international partner networks across medicine, public health, data science, social science and policy
  • Mentor and supervise doctoral, medical, master's and research students
  • Contribute to teaching activities in Gender Medicine, public health, data science or related areas
